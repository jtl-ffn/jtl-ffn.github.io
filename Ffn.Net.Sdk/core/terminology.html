Terminology

User

A registered user in the Fulfillment Network. Identified by a unique userId.

Merchant

A user who stores his products at a fulfillers warehouse and wants to ship orders to customers from there.

Fulfiller

A user with a warehouse who can store products and fulfill orders to customers.

Customer

The person who bought products from the merchant and will receive these products from the fulfiller.

Product

A good, that is to be warehoused by the fulfiller and later shipped to end customers.

Bill of materials

A special kind of product, that does not have stock itself, but contains other products.

Stock

The quantity of a product that exists in the network. There are different kinds of stock levels:

  • stockLevel - the amount of the given product the fulfiller has available in the respective warehouse
  • stockLevelAnnounced - the amount of the given product that is on it's way to the fulfiller (is in open inbounds)
  • stockLevelReserved - the amount of the stockLevel that is currently reserved by open outbounds
  • stockLevelBlocked - separate stock level of the product that is not available for shipment

Stock changes

Changes that occur in the stock level. These happen internally when:

  • An inbound is created by the merchant (stockLevelAnnounced will be increased)
  • An outbound is created by the merchant (stockLevelReserved will be increased)
  • An outbound shipping notification is created by the fulfiller (stockLevelReserved and stockLevel is decreased)
  • An adjustment is made by the fulfiller (stockLevel or stockLevelBlocked is increased/decreased)

Warehouse

A fulfillers warehouse that products can be sent to via inbounds and where products are later being shipped from to customers. Warehouses are created by fulfillers and can be authorized to merchants. Only authorized warehouses are visible to the respective merchant.

Shipping methods

A specific method of delivery of an order to the customer. A fulfiller manages the methods, that is is able to provide. Shipping methods need to be authorized to a merchants warehouse for the merchant to be able to use these shipping methods in order creation.

Inbound

A purchase order of goods being shipped by the merchant to one of his authorized warehouses. Inbounds represent an intent to ship and contain no actual shipment information.

Inbound shipping notification

Shipping information regarding an inbound. An inbound shipping notification tells the fulfiller what has been shipped when in which packages.

Outbound

An order that is to be fulfilled to the customer by the fulfiller.

Outbound shipping notification

Shipping information about products regarding an outbound that has left the fulfillers warehouse. It contains the products that were shipped and information regarding delivery.

Authorizations

There are several kinds of authorizations that allow merchants and fulfillers to work with each other:

  • User authorizations - Connect a merchant to a fulfiller.
  • Warehouse authorizations - A fulfiller grants a merchant the rights to ship to/from this warehouse
  • Shipping method authorizations - A fulfiller grants a merchant the right to ship with the given shipping method from a warehouse
  • Product authorizations - A merchant grants a fulfiller the right to see his product. These are also created automatically when an inbound is created.
